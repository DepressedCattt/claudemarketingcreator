## Scene Breakdown

### S1 — Hook (0f–95f, 3.2s)

**Text on screen:**
> "Your business is growing."
> "Your systems haven't caught up."

**Visual components:**
- **Revenue chart (left side):** An SVG line chart climbing upward from $0, showing
  business growth. The line draws on with a spring animation. Represents the
  positive side — the business IS growing.
- **Overdue task items (right side):** 4 task list items with red "OVERDUE" badges
  that cascade in with staggered timing. Items like "Follow up — Sarah M.",
  "Send invoice — Cedar project". Represents the negative side — systems failing.
- **Notification pills (upper right):** Small notification count badges (emails: 24,
  missed calls: 3, tasks: 12) that pop in. Shows the overwhelm.

**Emotional beat:** Recognition — "that's me, my business is growing but things are slipping"

**Transition out:** Cross-fade 7 frames, elements scale down slightly (0.97)

---

### S2 — Problem (88f–185f, 3.2s)

**Text on screen:**
> "5 tools. 12 tabs. Zero clarity."

**Visual components:**
- **Browser tab bar (top):** 5 browser tabs labeled "Google Sheets", "Gmail",
  "Trello", "WhatsApp", "Calendar" with colored favicons. They slide in from
  the left with staggered timing, physically crowding each other.
- **Mini app windows (center area):** 5 overlapping miniaturized application
  windows representing each tool:
  - Spreadsheet (green accent) — grid of cells with data
  - Email inbox (blue accent) — list of message rows with sender/subject
  - Chat app (purple accent) — message bubbles
  - Calendar (amber accent) — month grid with event dots
  - Notes app (rose accent) — lined text block
- Windows are slightly rotated (-3° to +3°) and overlapping to create a
  sense of visual chaos.

**Emotional beat:** Frustration — "this is exactly what my screen looks like"

**Transition out:** Cross-fade 7 frames

---

### S3 — The Shift (178f–265f, 2.9s)

**Text on screen:**
> "What if one platform replaced them all?"

**Visual components:**
- **Scattered app icons:** 5-6 small icons/fragments representing the scattered
  tools from S2, positioned randomly around the screen. They float briefly
  in their chaotic positions.
- **Convergence animation:** All icons spring toward the center of the screen
  simultaneously, converging into a single unified icon — the FlowPilot "FP"
  logo mark.
- **FlowPilot icon (center):** The unified brand icon that the fragments
  collapse into. Appears with a subtle glow pulse after convergence completes.
  Sits on a dark surface card.

**Emotional beat:** Hope — the satisfying "snap" of chaos becoming order

**Transition out:** Cross-fade 7 frames, the FP icon persists as the dashboard forms around it

### S4 — Product Reveal (258f–355f, 3.2s)

**Text on screen:**

**Visual components:**

- **Kanban board (main area):** 4 columns representing pipeline stages:

  - "Contacted" (2 cards) — similar cards
  - "Quoted" (1 card) — similar card
  - "Won" (1 card) — card with green accent
- **Client cards:** Each kanban card shows a client name and a brief service
  description, e.g., "Sarah Chen — Kitchen Renovation", "Mark Ellis — Bathroom
  Remodel". Cards spring in with staggered timing per column.

**Emotional beat:** Desire — "this looks clean, organized, I want this"

**Transition out:** Cross-fade 7 frames

### S5 — Features (348f–440f, 3.1s)

**Text on screen:**

## Pacing Notes

- **Acceleration:** S2 has rapid tab/window multiplication — each element enters fast
  to create a sense of overwhelming chaos.
- **Breathing room:** S4 product reveal holds longer — give the viewer time to absorb
  the clean dashboard after the chaos of S1-S3.
- **Climax:** S3 convergence is the peak moment — the satisfying snap of scattered
  icons collapsing into one. This is where the emotional shift happens.
- **Deceleration:** S7-S8 slow down. The tagline scene should feel conclusive and
  calm. The CTA is brief and confident.

## Visual Continuity

- Deep navy background (#0F172A) is consistent throughout — no palette shifts
- Brand blue (#3B82F6) appears as an accent in every scene: chart line, tab highlights,
  dashboard active state, stage connectors, metric card borders, CTA button
- The progression from chaotic multi-element scenes (S1-S2) to clean single-focus
  scenes (S7-S8) mirrors the chaos-to-control narrative
- Client name "Sarah Chen" appears in S4 kanban card, creating a subtle recurring detail

## CTA Specification

- **Brand name:** "FlowPilot" — 220px, weight 700, white with blue accent dot
- **Action:** "Try FlowPilot Free" — gradient button (brand blue → cyan)
- **Tagline echo:** "Less admin. More momentum." — 44px, weight 400, muted (#94A3B8)
