## Scene Breakdown

### S1 — Hook (0f–100f, 3.3s)

**Text on screen:**
> "Your clients can tell"
> "when you're winging it."

**Visual components:**
- **Client perspective mockup (center):** A phone/device frame showing
  a client's inbox with 3 messages from "Your Business":
  - "Re: Re: Re: Quote request" — 4 days ago, no attachment
  - "Sorry for the delay..." — 2 days ago
  - "Following up on my follow-up" — 1 day ago
  These represent the sloppy, repetitive communication clients actually see.
  Messages slide in with stagger, each one slightly embarrassing.
- **Subtle red indicators:** Small "unread" dots and time stamps in
  muted red/amber showing delays (4d, 2d, 1d).
- Text enters with blur-scale reveal above the phone mockup.

**Emotional beat:** A wince of recognition — "oh no, that IS what my clients see"

**Transition out:** Messages blur and scale down (0.97), cross-fade 8 frames

---

### S2 — The Cringe (95f–185f, 3.0s)

**Text on screen:**
> "Missed follow-ups. Lost details."
> "A great service, buried in bad admin."

**Visual components:**
- **Three client-experience "receipts" (horizontal row):** Small card
  panels showing what the client actually experiences:
  1. **Missed callback card:** Shows a phone icon with "Callback requested:
     Monday" and a red "Still waiting: Thursday" — 3-day gap highlighted.
  2. **Wrong details card:** A quote/invoice snippet showing the wrong
     client name crossed out and corrected — implies manual, error-prone
     processes.
  3. **Radio silence card:** A chat thread showing the client's message
     "Hi, any update on the project?" with a "Seen 3 days ago" indicator
     and no reply beneath it.
- Cards enter with staggered spring from below (left to right, 6f stagger).
  Each card has a subtle red left-border accent to signal "problem."

**Emotional beat:** Deepening discomfort — these aren't abstract problems,
they're specific embarrassments the viewer has probably caused

**Transition out:** Cards fade and drift down, cross-fade 8 frames

---

### S3 — The Pivot (180f–260f, 2.7s)

**Text on screen:**
> "Now imagine the opposite."

**Visual components:**
- **Visual shift:** The entire background transitions from the dark navy
  to a slightly lighter surface (#1E293B → a hint of soft gradient with
  blue accent glow) — signaling the emotional shift from cringe to
  aspiration. Not a full palette change, just a lift.
- **A single polished client notification (center):** A clean, modern
  notification card that the client would receive:
  - FlowPilot logo mark top-left
  - "Your project update is ready"
  - "View your project timeline →"
  - Timestamp: "Just now"
  - Clean design, electric blue accent, no clutter
  This represents what the client COULD see — automated, polished,
  immediate.
- The notification enters with a smooth spring from center-scale (0.92 → 1),
  replacing the chaotic cards from S2.

**Emotional beat:** Aspiration — "that looks so much better"

**Transition out:** Notification holds, background continues to lift, cross-fade 8 frames

**Emotional beat:** Desire — "this is what I want my clients to experience"

## Pacing Notes

- **Deceleration curve:** This ad starts fast and urgent (S1-S2 cringe)
  then slows as it becomes aspirational (S3-S5). The viewer is hooked
  by discomfort, then given breathing room to desire the solution.
- **Information density:** S4 (client timeline) is the densest scene and
  gets the most time (3.5s) — it needs room for 5 items to enter.
- **Breathing room:** S3 is the emotional hinge — short text, one visual,
  let the shift register.
- **Climax:** S4 is the peak — seeing the polished client experience in
  full. This is the moment the viewer thinks "I want that."

## Visual Continuity

- Deep navy background (#0F172A) throughout, with S3 introducing a subtle
  gradient lift that persists through S4-S5
- Electric blue (#3B82F6) threads through every scene: S1 message
  timestamps, S3 notification accent, S4 timeline connectors, S5
  dashboard highlights, S6 tagline emphasis, S7 CTA button
- Red/amber appears ONLY in S1-S2 (problem scenes), never in S3+
  (solution scenes) — color signals the emotional shift
- The progression from "what the client sees" (S1-S4 external view) to
  "what you see" (S5 internal dashboard) is a deliberate perspective flip
